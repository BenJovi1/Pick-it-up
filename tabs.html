What are tabs?

Tabs are a simplified way of writing music for the guitar. They use numbers and lines to represent the strings and frets on the guitar.

The six horizontal lines represent the six strings of the guitar, with the bottom line representing the low e string and the top line representing the high e string.

The numbers on the lines indicate which fret to play on that string. For example, a "0" means to play the string open, a "1" means to play the first fret, a "2" means to play the second fret, and so on.

reading tabs

To read tabs, start by looking at the lines and numbers. Each line represents a string on the guitar, and the numbers indicate which fret to play on that string.

For example, if you see a "0" on the bottom line, it means to play the low e string open. If you see a "3" on the second line from the bottom, it means to play the a string at the third fret.

Tabs are typically read from left to right, so start at the left side and work your way to the right.
